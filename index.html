Frequently Asked Questions

Who should complete this study?

Only an adult 18 years old or older who lives in the household should complete this study.

How long will it take to answer the questions to see if any adult is selected?

Completing this will take less than 5 minutes of your time.

What is the Co-Immunity Study/Who is sponsoring this?

The Co-Immunity Study is selecting adults from Jefferson County to look at how COVID -19 (Coronavirus) infections have spread through our community. The study will test to find out if the person selected currently has the virus (active infection) or has had it in the past, even if they have no symptoms. The Christina Lee Brown Environme Institute at the University of Louisville is conducting this study.

Where can I get more information? Is this legitimate?

The website below is hosted by the University of Louisville where you can find additional information.

Do I have to do this?

Participation is voluntary, but your participation is important to the success of this study. This online step will first see if any adult here is selected to participate.

Will this information be kept private?

Yes, information collected will be kept private and confidential. The study is covered by a Certificate of Confidentiality. The researchers with this Certificate cannot disclose the information or documents to anyone else who is not connected with the research.

If I complete this online do I also need to complete the paper form?

No, you only need to complete this once. If you completed online, then you do not need to return the paper form.

If an adult in my household is selected, what is involved?

Those selected to be tested will be asked to go to a local testing location and be tested by giving a nasal swab (for active virus now) and a blood sample collected by a finger stick (testing if you have a past infection). More details are provided to those who are selected.
